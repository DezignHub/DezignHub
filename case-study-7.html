Designing a Restaurant Management Platform

An end-to-end solution streamlining operations from reservations to inventory for restaurant owners

View Case Study See Prototype

Project Overview

Restaurant owners juggle multiple systems daily - reservations, orders, staff scheduling, and inventory management. This platform consolidates all operations into one intuitive interface.

The Challenge

Restaurant managers waste hours switching between disconnected systems, leading to operational inefficiencies and frustrated staff.

The Solution

A unified dashboard with real-time data synchronization across all restaurant operations, accessible on any device.

The Impact

Pilot restaurants reported 30% time savings on administrative tasks and 15% reduction in food waste through better inventory tracking.

User Research & Discovery

I conducted in-depth interviews with 12 restaurant owners and managers across different establishment sizes to understand pain points in their daily operations.

80% reported using 3+ different software systems daily

65% struggled with inventory tracking leading to waste

90% wanted mobile access to their management tools

Wireframing & Prototyping

After mapping core user journeys, I created low-fidelity wireframes to test workflow concepts with restaurant staff. The feedback informed several iterations before moving to high-fidelity designs.

Key Insights:

  • Need for customizable dashboard views based on staff roles
  • Critical importance of offline functionality for reliability
  • Integration with existing POS systems was non-negotiable

Final Design

The platform was designed with modular components that restaurants could configure based on their specific needs.

Unified Dashboard

Real-time overview of all restaurant operations with customizable widgets.

Inventory Tracking

Automated alerts when stock runs low with supplier integration.

Staff Management

Drag-and-drop scheduling with conflict detection.

View Interactive Prototype

Key Takeaways

This project reinforced the importance of understanding domain-specific workflows when designing for specialized industries like restaurant management.

Context Matters

Designing for high-stress environments requires special attention to error prevention and recovery.

Modular Approach

Building with configurable components allowed customization for different restaurant types.

Mobile First

Restaurant managers needed access on the go, requiring a responsive design that worked on any device.
