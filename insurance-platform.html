Insurance Platform UX Case Study

Redesigning the insurance experience with intuitive quoting, claims, and agent tools

Project Overview

The Challenge

Insurance platforms often overwhelm users with complex forms and unclear processes, leading to high abandonment rates during quoting and claims.

The Goal

Create a seamless, intuitive experience that simplifies insurance processes while maintaining compliance and security requirements.

My Role

Lead UX Designer responsible for research, user flows, wireframing, prototyping, and usability testing across all platform components.

Platform Components

Quoting System

Streamlined process with smart defaults and progressive disclosure

Claims Portal

Guided claims submission with document capture and status tracking

Agent Tools

Dashboard with client management and performance analytics

Key Findings

Complex Forms

72% of users abandoned the quoting process due to form length and unclear questions

Status Anxiety

Claims filers wanted more frequent updates and clearer timelines

Mobile Gaps

Document upload was particularly difficult on mobile devices

Key Design Decisions

Progressive Disclosure

Complex forms were broken into simple steps with contextual help. Only relevant questions were shown based on previous answers.

Document Capture

Mobile-optimized document upload with automatic cropping and quality checking to reduce claim submission errors.

Agent Dashboard

Customizable dashboard with quick access to common tasks, client alerts, and performance metrics.
