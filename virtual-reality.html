CASE STUDY

VR/AR Learning Platform

Designing an immersive educational platform that leverages virtual and augmented reality to enhance learning outcomes and engagement.

Project Overview

The VR/AR Learning Experience was designed to address the challenges of remote learning and engagement in complex subjects. Traditional e-learning platforms struggle with maintaining student attention and effectively teaching spatial concepts.

Our solution combines VR for immersive simulations and AR for real-world application, creating a blended learning environment that improves knowledge retention and practical skills.

Project Details

Duration

6 months

Team

UX Designer (me), 2 Developers, Product Manager

My Role

User Research, Interaction Design, Prototyping, User Testing

The Challenge

How might we create an immersive learning experience that improves engagement and knowledge retention for complex subjects while being accessible to students with varying technical skills?

Virtual Reality Augmented Reality Education Technology User Experience

Research & Discovery

User Interviews

Conducted interviews with 15 students and 8 educators to understand pain points in current learning methods.

Competitive Analysis

Analyzed 7 existing VR/AR education platforms to identify gaps and opportunities.

Academic Research

Reviewed studies on VR/AR learning effectiveness and cognitive load theory.

Key Findings

1

Engagement Drops

Students reported 42% drop in engagement after 20 minutes of traditional e-learning.

2

Spatial Learning

Complex spatial concepts were most challenging to teach through 2D interfaces.

3

Technical Barriers

60% of students were intimidated by complex VR setups but interested in the concept.

4

Collaboration Needs

Educators wanted tools for real-time collaboration in virtual spaces.

Wireframes & Prototyping

Early wireframes focused on simplifying the interface to reduce cognitive load while maintaining the immersive quality of the experience.

We conducted 3 rounds of usability testing with paper prototypes before moving to digital mockups.

Key Design Decisions

Visual Hierarchy

Prioritized 3D content with minimal UI elements to maintain immersion.

Intuitive Navigation

Gaze-based selection with controller backup for accessibility.

Social Features

Avatar-based collaboration tools for group learning.

Final Designs

VR Molecular Viewer

Students can manipulate complex molecular structures in 3D space with annotation tools.

AR Field Guide

Mobile AR application for identifying and learning about objects in the real world.

Educator Dashboard

Instructors can monitor student progress, create assignments, and host virtual classes.

Results & Impact

87%

Engagement Increase

Compared to traditional e-learning platforms in controlled studies.

42%

Retention Improvement

Higher test scores on spatial concept assessments after 2 weeks.

94%

Satisfaction Rate

Students reported they would recommend the platform to peers.

Key Takeaways

Onboarding is Critical

Simplifying the initial setup process dramatically improved adoption rates.

Balance Immersion & Comfort

Session length limits and comfort features reduced VR fatigue.

Blended Approach Works Best

Combining VR for immersion with AR for application created a complete learning cycle.

Educator Tools Matter

Teacher adoption was just as important as student adoption for institutional success.

Next Steps

This project demonstrated the potential of immersive technologies in education. Future iterations will explore adaptive learning paths, expanded subject areas, and improved accessibility features.
