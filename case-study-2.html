Case Study

Improving Patient-Doctor Communication Through Digital Innovation

A healthcare platform designed to streamline communication, reduce wait times, and improve patient outcomes.

View Prototype

Project Overview

The Challenge

Patients reported frustration with long wait times and difficulty communicating with healthcare providers between visits.

The Solution

A secure messaging platform with AI-assisted symptom tracking and automated appointment updates.

The Impact

Reduced phone inquiries by 40% and improved patient satisfaction scores by 35% in pilot clinics.

Understanding the Problem Space

Through interviews with both patients and healthcare providers, we identified key pain points in current communication channels:

  • 72% of patients felt their questions weren't fully answered during visits
  • Doctors spent 25% of their time on administrative communication
  • Critical test results were often delayed reaching patients

Design Process

Discovery & Research

Conducted 30+ interviews with patients, doctors, and administrative staff to map current workflows and pain points.

Wireframing & Prototyping

Developed low-fidelity prototypes tested with 5 medical practices to validate core functionality.

User Testing & Iteration

Conducted 3 rounds of testing with patients and doctors to refine the interface and workflows.

Key Features

Secure Messaging

HIPAA-compliant direct messaging between patients and their care team with read receipts and priority tagging.

Appointment Management

Automated reminders, easy rescheduling, and waitlist notifications to reduce no-shows by 28%.

Health Tracking

Patients can log symptoms and vital signs that automatically populate their medical records.

Test Results

Automated notifications when results are ready with physician notes and follow-up actions.

Key Learnings

This project reinforced several important lessons about designing for healthcare:

  • Security is non-negotiable

    Every design decision needed to prioritize patient privacy and data security.

  • Both sides matter

    Solutions must work for overburdened providers as well as anxious patients.

  • Implementation takes time

    Changing workflows in healthcare requires extensive training and gradual adoption.

Next Steps

Expanding to specialty care and integrating with more EHR systems to reach additional patient populations.
